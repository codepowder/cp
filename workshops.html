KIDS' VIDEO GAME WORKSHOPS

VIDEO GAMES HELP CHILDREN TO LEARN, TO COMMUNICATE, AND TO STIR THEIR IMAGINATION.

From 1st to 4th July 2019, 17 young attendees, ages 8 to 12, had great fun building and publishing their very own 2D platform video games, at the Art Kino in Rijeka, Croatia.



The children created their first video games using the powerful game engine known as Unity.

 

 




And published their creations on the very popular Itch gaming website, to share with their friends.

 

 

NO CODING

We re-structured and simplified a Unity Playground project for the children to use as a starting point, with which they could build their game without writing a single line of code.

SIMPLIFIED INTERFACE

The Playground project enabled us to make the user interface simpler. This made teaching children how to use the software much easier. It also gave them more freedom to be creative with minimum input from our instructor. Which meant the instructor could devote more time to each attendee.

FLOW CREATIVELY

With each day's session the kids were guided through creating their game step by step. They started by creating their character and making it move. And then began to shape their level. Followed by adding obstacles and enemies. And finally wrapping up and testing their games together.


Art-Kino organised the video game workshops alongside other activities for children of all ages ...

 

 



... as part of the
children's Tobogan Festival,
held in Rijeka, Croatia, from
26th June to 10th July 2019.
